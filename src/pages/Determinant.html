Notice the pattern where entries in a row act as scalars to the determinants of the matrices of the rows below it.

  • A matrix and scalar pair can be found by eliminating the column and row of the scalar.
  • Each pair produces terms (because a determinant produces two terms, a x determinant produces terms and so on)

Note: it is possible to use cofactor expansion where the scalars are on a column, but the matrix must be transposed.

The terms of a determinant calculation using Laplace expansion (cofactor expansion) are used as the elements of a [[Cross Product ||cross product]] where the first row are the basis and each subsequent row is the vector of the cross product equation (read left to right).

Geometric Interpretation

  • For a x matrix, the unsigned determinant represents the area of the parallelogram formed by the column vectors that make up the matrix.
  • For a x matrix, the unsigned determinant is the volume of the parallelepiped formed by the bases of the matrix.
  • And so on.
