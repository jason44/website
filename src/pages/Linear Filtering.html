Also known as cross-correlation or simply, a convolution. Each point of the new image is a [[linear combination]] of its neighbors.

For example, this kernel is a linear filter known as the 3x3 mean averaging kernel because each point takes the mean value of the surrounding pixels. If we were to replace the mean distribution with a gaussian distribution, we would get what is known as a gaussian blur.

This kernel obviously results in an identical image This kernel shifts the image by 1 pixel left This kernel has the affect of sharpening the image because each point on the edge of a foreground is accentuated while pixels inside or outside a foreground don't change as drastically
