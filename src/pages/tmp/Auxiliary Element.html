When solving a problem it may be beneficial to introduce a new element in hopes that it may further the endeavor.

Types of Auxiliary Elements

  • In a geometric problem, we may introduce auxiliary lines into the figure.
  • In an algebraic problem, we may introduce an auxiliary unknown.
  • An auxiliary theorem is a theorem we prove in hopes that it can be useful for solving the problem at hand.

Trying known results and going back to definitions are both often used auxiliary element.

For example, in algebra we often remove the radical from a denominator by introducing an auxiliary element.

Auxiliary Problem

A problem we solve in hopes that it will help us in solving the problem at hand.
